Linear & Logistic Regression Models

My approach was an exercise in not over-engineering, over-fitting your data and understanding, that Data Science, like other sciences, sometimes requires experimentation.

Following a failed attempt at fitting our limited data to a Deep Learning model I scrapped my initial code, and went back to the drawing board. I then began implementing the most straightforward ML model, a Linear Regression, in order to see if there was even a correlation between the Stock performance data and the Tweet sentiment score.

My four choices for stocks were all in the transportation industry, with companies that had been in the news without Trump's calling to attention, as well as two other stocks that were not often in the news. The stocks were American Airlines Grp(A1G.F), Boeing Aerospace(BA), CSX Rail(CSX), and General Motors(GM)

Below are the plots for the Linear models for the respective stocks.



As the above graphs illustrate, data points for the training and the test data almost perfectly aline, however, as the model scores show, the data does not accurately fit the model.


Since the plots for the test and training data correlated, but the models' accuracy scores did not provide coroborration for the model statiscally, and moreover, since our goals were to see if we could predict the stocks' behavior and label it in a binary format of either "Positive" or "Negative" change following the tweets sentiment score, I chose to implement a Logistic model as well.
